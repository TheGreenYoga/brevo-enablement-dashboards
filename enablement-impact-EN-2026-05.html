Key findings

📉 CSM contact: −13.9 pts — participants are more autonomous

Participants contacted their CSM at 56.1% vs 70.0% for the control. Even more striking: their last CSM touch was 65d ago on average, vs 32d for non-participants — a 33-day gap that signals real behavioural independence.

🎫 Ticket rate: +9.9 pts — more proactive self-service

Participants open tickets at 31.6% vs 21.7% for the control. More ticket usage after attending a session suggests participants know how to route their own issues rather than going straight to their CSM.

▶️ Replay watchers more autonomous than live attendees

Replay viewers show lower CSM contact (44.4% vs 62.5% for live) and higher MRR (€896 vs €786). Likely selection bias: larger/busier accounts watch on their own schedule — but it's a signal worth tracking.

⚠️ T0 health gap: selection bias risk

Participants started with a higher health baseline (T0: 5.47 vs 4.49). Healthier accounts may be more likely to self-select into webinars, which inflates the apparent impact. A matched-health control group would give a cleaner read in the next iteration.

Methodology & caveats
⚠️ Read before citing these numbers externally.
  • Participants: 40 attended live + 18 watched replay between 2026-03-01 and 2026-05-31. Internal @brevo.com accounts excluded.
  • Control: 207 Pro EN Planhat customers (status=customer) not appearing in the participant list.
  • Touch window: 90 days ending 2026-05-31, applied identically to both groups.
  • CSM contact proxy: binary — any email/call/note touch in Planhat in window.
  • Ticket proxy: binary — any ticket touch in Planhat in window. Not a full Zendesk count.
  • T0 health proxy: h − hDiff (state before last health change, not necessarily before the session date).
  • ⚠ Participants had higher T0 health (5.47 vs 4.49) — selection bias likely.
  • ⚠ CSM contact rate includes all email touches — automated sequences (onboarding flows, NPS, etc.) are indistinguishable from manual CSM emails in Planhat. Call and note touches are always manual.
