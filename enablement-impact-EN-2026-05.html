Enablement Sessions Impact — EN

2026-03-01 → 2026-05-31  ·  57 participants  ·  207 control  ·  Generated 2026-06-17

Hypothesis: Customers who attend Brevo enablement sessions are more autonomous — they contact their CSM less frequently, rely more on self-service support, and maintain comparable or higher health scores.
Methodology & Caveats
  • Participants: Livestorm registrants who attended live (46) or watched a replay (22) between 2026-03-01 and 2026-05-31 — internal @brevo.com accounts excluded
  • Control: Pro EN Planhat customers (status=customer) who did not appear in participant list — 207 accounts
  • Touch window: 90 days ending 2026-05-31, applied identically to both groups
  • CSM contact proxy: binary — any email/call/note touch recorded in Planhat in window
  • Ticket proxy: binary — any support ticket touch recorded in Planhat in window
  • T0 health proxy: h − hDiff (pre-last-change baseline, not exact session date)
  • Ο Participants had higher T0 health (5.47 vs 4.49) — selection bias: healthier accounts may be more likely to attend webinars
  • Ο Control group includes 27 Onboarding accounts (13%) which naturally show higher CSM contact — S&G-only view corrects for this
  • Ο Automated email workflows may inflate email touch rate in the control group

Key Findings

CSM Contact: -13.9 pts — participants are more autonomous

Participants had a CSM contact rate of 56.1% vs 70.0% for non-participants. The S&G-only view sharpens this: 57.1% vs 79.6% (−22.5 pts), ruling out the Onboarding phase as the sole driver. This is the clearest signal of increased autonomy in the dataset.

Ticket Rate: +8.5 pts — more self-service channel usage

Participants open tickets at 29.8% vs 21.3% for the control. Higher ticket usage after attending a session suggests participants know how to find answers proactively, rather than contacting their CSM directly for every issue.

Health Score: 5.54 vs 5.97

Participants start from a higher baseline (T0: 5.47 vs 4.49) and maintain their score post-session (hDiff: +0.07). The control group shows more momentum (+1.48) largely driven by 27 onboarding accounts with fast-climbing health scores. ⚠ The T0 gap suggests selection bias: higher-health accounts may be more likely to attend sessions.

MRR Profile: €834 vs €682 (+€152)

Participants average €834 MRR vs €682 for the control — a 22% premium. Higher MRR accounts attending sessions reinforces the ROI case for customer enablement programs. Consider segmenting by MRR band in future runs to isolate the effect from size.
