Key findings

📉 CSM contact −12.7 pts — autonomy signal

60% of participants had any CSM contact vs 72.7% of the control group. The call+note signal (always manual) also dropped: 10% vs 13.9% — independent of automated email noise. Both measures point in the same direction.

🎫 Ticket rate +8.7 pts — self-service confirmed

36% of participants opened a support ticket vs 27.3% of the control. This is the cleanest signal available — no ambiguity around automated sequences. Participants navigate the product independently and route themselves to support rather than their CSM.

📅 +47d since last touch

Participants were last contacted an average of 69d ago vs 22d for the control. The gap is partly amplified by the window end date (contacts after May 31 not counted), but direction is consistent with other signals.

⚠️ Selection bias at T0 — worth noting

Participants start with a lower health score (T0: 5.30 vs 4.77). Accounts with difficulties are more likely to be directed to enablement sessions. This bias works against participants on health comparisons — yet CSM and ticket signals remain favorable despite this baseline disadvantage.

Methodology & caveats
⚠️ Directional signals only — not causal evidence.
  • Participants: 42 live + 9 replay, Mar–May 2026. @brevo.com accounts excluded. n=50 = 35 direct customers + 15 Enterprise sub-accounts (BU / divisions of existing customer groups).
  • Control: 209 Pro FR Planhat customers (status=customer), excluding participant domains.
  • Contact window: Mar 1 – May 31 2026 (90 days), applied identically to both groups.
  • CSM contact proxy: binary (yes/no per company, not a count). Three measures: total, email only, call+note only.
  • ⚠ CSM email touch includes automated sequences — indistinguishable from manual emails in Planhat. Call and note touches are always manual.
  • * MRR shown on the 35 direct customers only. The 15 sub-accounts carry MRR=0 in Planhat (revenue tracked at parent account level).
  • T0 health = h − hDiff (proxy — not the exact value at session date).
