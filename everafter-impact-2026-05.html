Key Findings
🚫 Zero Churn Among EverAfter Users
0 churns among the 58 clients who completed at least one EverAfter stage. Groups B and C show 3.3% and 2.6% respectively. The signal is directionally strong even at this sample size.
🤝 Less Manual CSM Work
Call+note rate: 19.0% (A) vs 25.0% (B) and 22.1% (C). The S&G-only view confirms: 12.1% vs 18.8% (B). EverAfter users in mature phases still require 6 pts less hands-on effort.
🎫 Ticket Pattern: Group B is the Friction Signal
Group B (opened but no stage) has the highest ticket rate at 6.5% — nearly double Group A (3.4%) and 5× Group C (1.3%). This suggests clients who start EverAfter but don't complete stages get stuck and escalate to support.
⚠️ The Activation Gap Remains the Main Lever
Only 25.6% of new Pro clients engage with EverAfter stages. 40.5% open it but complete nothing. Converting Group B clients into Group A is the highest-impact intervention — and the ticket data suggests they need a push.

📍 Next Steps

  • Investigate the Group B friction signal: why do 40% open EverAfter but complete no stage? Is the first stage too long, unclear, or irrelevant?
  • Re-run in 3 months on the same cohort for longitudinal health and churn tracking
  • Share Group B ticket spike with the EverAfter product team — it points to onboarding friction at stage 1
  • Cross-segment by CSM: are some CSMs driving better EverAfter activation than others?
  • Share Group A zero-churn data with leadership as EverAfter ROI narrative
